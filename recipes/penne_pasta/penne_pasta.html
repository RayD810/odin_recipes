Penne Pasta with Pesto Sauce

Description

Penne Pasta coated in pesto sauce with tomatos, zuchinni, and asparagus - garnished with sliced basil. The perfect date dish when you're crunched for time and ready to eat

Ingredients

  • 1 Tbsp Oil
  • Salt (to taste)
  • 1 lb large chicken breast, cooked and diced
  • 2 cups asparagus, cut into 1 1/2-in 38mm pieces
  • 10 oz cherry tomatos, halved
  • 2/3 cup pesto
  • 2 cups whole wheat penne
  • Sliced basil for a garnish

Directions

  1. Cook pasta in salted water according to the package instructions, take it out at “al dente” as you will cook it more in the next steps. Tip: I’m using rotisserie chicken in this recipe, but if you would like to use a raw chicken breast then cube it, toss in jerk seasoning and cook until it’s golden and cooked through. Then remove from the pot, and set aside to use later.
  2. Prep the veggies: Slice the bell peppers (you can use different colors of peppers), green onions, and garlic.
  3. Add vegetable oil to a heavy-bottomed pot, and saute bell peppers with green onions. Add the minced garlic and cook until it’s fragrant.
  4. Add jerk seasoning to the pot.
  5. Add the heavy cream, and chicken or vegetable stock to the pot. Simmer for a few minutes or until the sauce thickens.
  6. Add parmesan cheese to the pot, and stir until it melts in the sauce. Then finally add the chicken pieces and pasta and give everything a good mix so the ingredients are well coated in the sauce.
  7. Garnish with chopped chives or parsley and serve.

    8. Mixture can be stored for up to 4 days.

  8. Enjoy!
